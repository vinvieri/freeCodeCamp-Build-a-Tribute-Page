Timeline of Steve Job's life:

  • 1955 – Steve Jobs is born in San Francisco, California to Joanne Schieble and Abdulfattah "John" Jandali. He is adopted by Paul and Clara Jobs.
  • 1972 – Jobs enrolls at Reed College in Portland, Oregon but drops out after one semester, though he continues auditing classes.
  • 1974 – Jobs works at Atari as a video game designer. He also joins the Homebrew Computer Club, where he meets Steve Wozniak and they begin collaborating.
  • 1976 – Jobs and Wozniak co-found Apple Computer and release the Apple I personal computer. They sell the first 50 units to a local dealer.
  • 1977 – Apple releases the more advanced Apple II, which becomes a major success and catapults the company to prominence in the personal computer market.
  • 1979 – Jobs visits Xerox PARC and is inspired by the Xerox Alto's graphical user interface, which he incorporates into the design of the forthcoming Macintosh.
  • 1983 – Jobs lures John Sculley from Pepsi to become Apple's CEO, famously asking "Do you want to sell sugar water for the rest of your life, or do you want to come with me and change the world?"
  • 1984 – Apple introduces the Macintosh, the first personal computer with a graphical user interface, to great acclaim.
  • 1985 – After a power struggle with the Apple board, Jobs is forced out of the company he co-founded.
  • 1986 – Jobs buys The Graphics Group (later renamed Pixar) from Lucasfilm's computer graphics division for $10 million.
  • 1996 – Apple acquires NeXT, the company founded by Jobs, and brings him back to the company in a leadership role.
  • 1997 – Jobs becomes interim CEO of Apple and helps revive the company, which had been struggling.
  • 2001 – Apple introduces the iPod music player, which becomes hugely successful and helps establish Apple as a major consumer electronics company.
  • 2007 – Apple introduces the iPhone, which revolutionizes the smartphone industry.
  • 2011 – Steve Jobs passes away at the age of 56 after a long battle with his illness.
